# DegreeZero — Outreach & Distribution Playbook

A practical guide for releasing DegreeZero: who to contact, how, what to send, and
how to spread it. Built for **nonpartisan credibility** — the strategy mirrors the
product: reach across the spectrum, lead with method and sources, never spin.

> **Verify before sending.** Beats, handles, and tip lines change. Confirm each
> contact's current role and preferred channel before you reach out. Treat the
> named individuals below as *examples of the right beat*, and prefer official
> outlet tip lines / SecureDrop over guessed personal emails.

---

## 0. Positioning (say this the same way every time)

**One-liner:** "DegreeZero is a nonpartisan, open-source platform that scores what
public officials actually did — and whether it worked — measured against each
action's own stated goal, with every number linked to its official government
source and every score independently reproducible."

**Why it's different (the hook):**
- Not an ideological scorecard — **procedurally neutral**, party-blind code.
- **Pre-registered** method (metric + baseline hashed before outcomes are seen).
- **"Insufficient evidence" instead of a fake number** when a case can't be isolated.
- **Fully reproducible** + append-only audit log; anyone can re-derive any score.
- **Two layers:** "what they acted on" (record) and "did it work" (scored).

Keep these five bullets identical across every email, DM, and deck. Consistency is
credibility.

## 1. The pitch email (template)

> **Subject:** Nonpartisan, reproducible scoring of what officials did — and whether it worked
>
> Hi [name],
>
> I built DegreeZero, a nonpartisan, open-source platform that measures public
> officials' actions against their *own stated goals* using only official
> government data — and reports "insufficient evidence" rather than guessing when an
> effect can't be isolated. The scoring code never sees party, the method is
> pre-registered, and every score is reproducible bit-for-bit with a full source
> trail.
>
> It might be useful for [specific beat/story angle]. Two-layer view: what an
> official *acted on* (sponsored/cosponsored bills, votes, executive orders) and,
> separately, *did it work* (outcome vs. a pre-declared baseline).
>
> Live demo: [URL] · One-page method: [link to WHITEPAPER.md] · Full methodology &
> code: [repo link]. Happy to walk through the methodology or pull a custom view for
> a state/official you cover.
>
> Best,
> [you] · [contact]

Attach/links: the **white paper** (`docs/WHITEPAPER.md`), the **methodology**
(`docs/METHODOLOGY.md`), a 60–90s screen capture, and 2–3 example official pages
(pick a mix of parties to demonstrate neutrality).

## 2. Target list

> Reach across the spectrum on purpose. Pitching left, right, and center outlets
> *equally* is itself evidence of the product's neutrality — lead with that.

### 2.1 Data / accountability journalism desks (highest priority)
These desks live for reproducible, source-anchored datasets.
- **ProPublica** — data & politics desk; public tips page / SecureDrop.
- **The New York Times** — Upshot (data/politics) and politics tips line.
- **The Washington Post** — Fact Checker; data team; politics tips.
- **Reuters / AP** — data journalism and fact-check teams (wire pickup = scale).
- **The Markup / CalMatters / Texas Tribune / Stateline** — civic + state-level data.
- **FiveThirtyEight-style data writers** (now dispersed across outlets/newsletters).
- **Bloomberg / Axios** — politics + data verticals (Axios = concise, shareable).
- **NPR** — data journalism + member-station investigative desks.
- **BBC / Guardian US** — data teams (international reach, US politics).

### 2.2 Fact-checking organizations
- **PolitiFact**, **FactCheck.org (Annenberg)**, **Washington Post Fact Checker**,
  **Snopes**, **AP Fact Check**, **Reuters Fact Check**, **Full Fact** (UK model).

### 2.3 Think tanks & policy shops (pitch ACROSS the spectrum)
- Center-left/centrist: **Brookings**, **Urban Institute**, **Bipartisan Policy
  Center**, **Niskanen Center**, **Center for American Progress**.
- Center-right/free-market: **American Enterprise Institute (AEI)**, **Cato
  Institute**, **Heritage Foundation**, **Manhattan Institute**, **R Street**.
- Process/democracy: **Brennan Center**, **Protect Democracy**, **Pew Research
  Center** (data culture fit), **Bipartisan Policy Center**.
- Pitch each the *method* and offer their analysts API access. Cross-spectrum
  adoption is the strongest possible neutrality signal.

### 2.4 Academics (credibility multipliers; great early reviewers)
- Political methodology / causal inference (synthetic control, DiD).
- Public-policy and public-administration evaluation faculty.
- Civic-tech and "open government data" researchers.
- Channels: department contact pages, faculty email, methods conferences (APSA,
  PolMeth, MPSA), and preprint/working-paper venues.

### 2.5 Civic-tech & open-government organizations (natural allies/integrators)
- **GovTrack**, **OpenSecrets (CRP)**, **Sunlight-legacy** community, **Code for
  America** brigades, **Ballotpedia**, **Vote Smart**, **USAFacts**, **Open States
  / Plural**, **mySociety** (international). Offer data interop and cross-links.

### 2.6 Podcasts & video (explain the method, reach engaged audiences)
- Data/forecasting & politics-by-the-numbers shows.
- Civic-minded interview shows across the spectrum (book one left-leaning, one
  right-leaning, one centrist to model neutrality).
- Tech/founder shows (the open-source + reproducibility angle).
- YouTube explainer channels on civics/government and on data science.

### 2.7 Newsletters & independent writers (fast, high-trust distribution)
- Substack writers covering politics-by-data, civic tech, and "how government
  works." Independent newsletters convert curiosity into recurring coverage.

### 2.8 Officials, staff & institutions (use carefully, nonpartisan)
- **Committee/member offices** you've scored — offer them their own page and a
  correction/dispute channel *before* publicity (good faith + reduces pushback).
- **State legislatures / governors' offices** for state-policy scores.
- **Nonpartisan institutions**: GAO, CBO, CRS culture (data-rigor audiences),
  NCSL, NGA, the Volcker Alliance, the National Institute for Civil Discourse.
- Keep all outreach identical regardless of party — and say so.

### 2.9 Advocacy, networks & conveners (amplification, label clearly)
- Cross-partisan reform networks: **Issue One**, **RepresentUs**, **Unite
  America**, **With Honor**, **No Labels**-style centrist groups, **Stand Together**
  (right), and good-government chapters (**League of Women Voters**, **Common
  Cause**). Accept amplification but never co-branding that implies endorsement.

### 2.10 Lobbyists & government-relations (context, not credibility)
- GR firms and trade associations are *consumers* of objective records. Useful for
  reach into official circles, but never let them shape the method or messaging.

## 3. Contact methods (in order of preference)
1. **Outlet tip lines / SecureDrop** (e.g. each major outlet's `/tips` page) — most
   reliable, on-the-record-friendly.
2. **A specific reporter on the right beat**, referencing a recent relevant story.
3. **General/data-desk inboxes** when no individual fits.
4. **Public DMs/mentions** as a warm-up, not a primary channel.
5. **Press release on the wire** (PR Newswire / EIN Presswire) for broad pickup.

---

## 4. Launch sequence (suggested)
1. **Pre-brief (week −1):** 3–5 trusted data journalists + 2–3 academics under a
   short embargo. Ask for methodology critique; fix anything they break.
2. **Notify scored offices (week −1):** give them their pages + the dispute channel.
3. **Launch day:** publish white paper + demo; send the tier-1 desk emails; post a
   concise thread (the five bullets + one striking, *clearly-caveated* example with
   sources).
4. **Week +1:** podcasts/newsletters; offer think tanks API access; publish a
   "how it works" explainer and the reproducibility self-audit results.
5. **Ongoing:** a "Recently scored" cadence; respond to every dispute publicly with
   the deterministic re-run diff (turns scrutiny into proof of integrity).

---

## 5. Do / don't
- **Do** lead with method, sources, and reproducibility; show both parties; invite
  scrutiny; publish corrections loudly.
- **Do** offer custom pulls (a state, an official, a topic) — reporters love a
  ready-made angle.
- **Don't** claim a single number is the whole truth; emphasize the factual vector
  + confidence.
- **Don't** accept framing or co-branding that implies partisan endorsement.
- **Don't** overstate coverage — be explicit about the scored vs. record layers and
  about "insufficient evidence."

---

## 6. Assets to have ready before outreach
- Live demo URL + 2–3 example official pages (mixed parties).
- `docs/WHITEPAPER.md` (this is your shareable one-pager) and `docs/METHODOLOGY.md`.
- A 60–90s screen recording and 3–4 static screenshots (incl. an "insufficient
  evidence" example to show the honesty mechanism).
- The reproducibility self-audit output and the public dispute flow.
- A short FAQ: "Is this partisan?", "How is a score computed?", "Why is X
  'insufficient evidence'?", "Can I check the math?".

each audience a ready-made angle with sources, and let anyone re-derive every
number themselves.*
